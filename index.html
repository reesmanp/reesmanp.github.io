About

I work best in the space between engineering, product, customers, and the people who need the software to work when it matters.

That has meant different things at different stages of my career: co-founding and architecting secure program management software for aerospace and defense; designing FedRAMP and GovCloud migration paths; building HIPAA-compliant patient data systems; improving high-traffic eCommerce platforms; creating machine-learning recommendation systems for news; and leading engineering teams through the less glamorous but critical work of security, compliance, reliability, and scale.

I'm comfortable going deep technically, but I'm just as interested in making the technical work legible: to customers, executives, product teams, auditors, engineers, and anyone else who needs to make a decision. I like diagrams, tradeoffs, working demos, clear docs, and blunt conversations about what a system can and cannot do.

Before software, I served as a Forward Observer in 2nd Ranger Battalion. That experience still shapes how I work: plan carefully, communicate clearly, adapt quickly, and stay calm when the plan changes.

Useful when the problem includes

  • Complex technical discovery
  • Customer-facing architecture
  • Regulated and secure environments
  • Engineering tradeoffs that need executive clarity
  • A demo that has to become a real implementation
  • Explainable, deployable, defensible systems
